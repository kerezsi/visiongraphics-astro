We work with architects, developers, investors, and project teams at different stages of the same projects. The 3D model and the visualization work are the same — what changes is what the output needs to accomplish.

## For Architects

A 2D drawing communicates to other architects. It doesn't communicate to clients, planners, or stakeholders who aren't trained to read plans. Visualization bridges that gap.

We help architects:
- Show clients exactly what they're getting, before they've committed to it
- Identify design conflicts early — spatial issues that aren't obvious in plan become visible in 3D
- Win competitions with clear, compelling visual communication
- Explain complex details — structural systems, circulation, phasing — to non-technical audiences

Typical timeline: 3–4 weeks from complete design materials to finished visualization package.

## For Developers

Pre-selling units before construction begins — that's where visualization pays for itself most clearly. Planning permissions, investor presentations, sales office setups, marketing campaigns: all of this runs on visualization.

We help developers:
- Move units before groundbreaking with photorealistic marketing materials
- Accelerate planning approval with visuals that make the project legible to committees
- Build phased packages aligned to sales launch schedules
- Set up sales environments — print, web, interactive, VR

Typical timeline: 6–8 weeks for a full marketing visualization package, staged to match your launch sequence.

## For Investors

Before committing capital, you need to understand what you're actually funding. Visualization provides that clarity — not just what the project looks like, but how it sits in context, what the scale and quality of spaces will be, how it compares to the brief.

We help investors:
- Understand spatial quality and scale before construction
- Spot risks early — design gaps, scale mismatches, context issues
- Communicate the vision clearly to co-investors, boards, and lenders
- Track design development across project phases

Typical timeline: 4–5 weeks for an investor-focused visualization package covering key decision points.
