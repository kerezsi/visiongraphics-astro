The most significant development in visualization in 30 years: we can now produce photorealistic images and animations from hand sketches, rough plans, or reference photos — without building a full 3D model first. What used to take weeks of modelling before a single render can now begin from a drawing.

This doesn't make 3D obsolete. For final delivery, detailed animation, and client-facing work, a proper 3D scene still produces results no AI can match. But for early-stage exploration, rapid client feedback, and concept approval — the process has fundamentally changed.

The workflow: you send us a sketch, reference image, or rough plan. We run it through controlled AI pipelines with the right prompting and ControlNet guidance to produce a photorealistic result that actually matches your design intent — not a hallucinated approximation. Where precision matters, we add targeted 3D elements and composite them in.

This is practical for:
- Early-stage client presentations before the 3D model exists
- Design competitions where speed matters
- Quick iteration on concept options for internal review
- Repurposing existing renders for new contexts or seasons

AI tools genuinely accelerate some parts of visualization work — early-stage exploration, batch processing, and image correction. They don't replace 30 years of scene-building knowledge or the precision a proper 3D render delivers at final quality. We use AI where it reduces time and cost without compromising what matters.

**Is AI right for your project?**

- Early-stage concept presentations: yes, significant time savings
- Final delivery renders for marketing and planning: usually no — 3D still wins on control and precision
- Large batches of repeat renders (variants, angles, seasons): yes, very effective
- One-off hero images: depends on complexity — we'll tell you honestly

We don't push AI on projects where it adds more correction time than it saves. If a straightforward 3D approach is faster, we'll say so.
