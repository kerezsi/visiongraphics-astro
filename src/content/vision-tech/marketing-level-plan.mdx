<h2>What's a Marketing Level Plan?</h2><p>Marketing level plans show entire floors or building levels in a visually appealing way that's easy for non-architects to understand. They illustrate how multiple spaces relate to each other, highlight common areas, and show the context of individual units within the larger building. These plans use color, shading, and simplified details to communicate clearly.</p> 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 <h2>Why Use It?</h2><p>Technical level plans are confusing for most people. Marketing level plans help when you need to:</p><ul><li>Show unit locations within a building</li><li>Highlight amenity spaces and circulation</li><li>Demonstrate views and orientations</li><li>Explain access and security zones</li><li>Create a consistent marketing system</li><li>Help buyers understand building context</li></ul><p>Clear level plans help people make confident buying decisions.</p><h2>How It Works</h2><p>We transform complex technical drawings through several steps:</p><ol><li><strong>Base Plan Simplification</strong> We remove technical details while keeping important elements</li><li><strong>Unit Identification</strong> We create a clear numbering/naming system and color code by unit type</li><li><strong>Feature Enhancement</strong> We emphasize common areas, views, and building features</li><li><strong>Orientation</strong> We add compass directions and context elements</li><li><strong>Visual Design</strong> We apply colors, shadows, and graphic elements to make information clear</li><li><strong>Integration</strong> We ensure level plans work with your other marketing materials</li></ol> 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 <h2>What Makes It Special</h2><h5>Building Context</h5><p>Level plans show how individual units fit into the larger project.</p><h5>Wayfinding</h5><p>These plans help people understand how to navigate the building.</p><h5>Decision Support</h5><p>Buyers can see relationships between units, amenities, and building features that affect their decision.</p><h5>Sales Tool</h5><p>Sales teams can use these plans to explain building features and help clients find the perfect unit.</p> 
 
 
 
 
  
 <h2>When to Use It</h2><p>Marketing level plans are valuable for:</p><ul><li>Project websites and interactive tools</li><li>Sales center displays</li><li>Marketing brochures</li><li>Building directories</li><li>Wayfinding systems</li><li>Construction phasing communication</li></ul> 
 
 
 
 <h3>Input Requirements</h3><ul><li>Architectural floor plans</li><li>Unit mix information</li><li>Amenity space details</li><li>View directions and features</li><li>Project branding guidelines</li></ul><h3>Output Options</h3><p>We deliver level plans in formats suitable for:</p><ul><li>Large format printing</li><li>Digital displays</li><li>Interactive applications</li><li>Print publications</li><li>Wayfinding systems</li></ul> 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 <h2>Common Questions</h2><p><strong>How detailed should level plans be?</strong> The right level of detail depends on your use case - we balance clarity with information density.</p><p><strong>Can you highlight different features for different audiences?</strong> Yes, we can create variations that emphasize different aspects of the same plan.</p><p><strong>How do you handle complex buildings?</strong> We use visual techniques like color coding, call-outs, and simplified geometry to make even complex layouts understandable.</p><p><strong>Can these be made interactive?</strong> Absolutely - we can prepare files for developers to create interactive versions where users can click on units for more information.</p>
