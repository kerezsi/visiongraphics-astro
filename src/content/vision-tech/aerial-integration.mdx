<h2>What is Aerial Integration?</h2><p>We take real aerial photos and add your building to them. Simple as that. It shows exactly how your project will fit into the neighborhood when it's done.</p> 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 <h2>Why Use Aerial Integration?</h2><p>Regular renderings can look great, but they don't show the surroundings right. Aerial integrations fix that problem:</p><ul><li>People see your building in its actual location</li><li>Shows the true scale compared to nearby buildings</li><li>Helps with planning approvals</li><li>Makes marketing more realistic</li><li>Lets you spot potential issues early</li></ul><p>Planning boards love these because they can see exactly how your building relates to everything around it.</p><h2>How We Create Them</h2><p>We have a few ways to get the base photos:</p><ol><li>Airplane photography - we work with experienced pilots who can capture the perfect angle</li><li>Helicopter shots - great for lower altitudes and precise positioning</li><li>Drone photos - perfect for smaller sites or specific angles</li><li>Google Earth - for initial studies in areas they cover in 3D</li></ol><p>Once we have the base image, we:</p><ul><li>Create a 3D model of your building</li><li>Match the exact perspective of the photo</li><li>Align the sunlight direction</li><li>Blend the shadows and reflections</li><li>Adjust the colors to match the photo's conditions</li></ul><p>The result looks natural because we're careful about these details.</p> 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 <h2>When to Use Aerial Integration</h2><p>This works best when:</p><ul><li>You need to show context and surroundings</li><li>Your project will change the neighborhood</li><li>You're presenting to planning boards</li><li>Marketing to investors or buyers</li><li>Working on larger developments</li></ul> 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 <h2>What You Get</h2><p>We deliver:</p><ul><li>High-resolution images ready for any use</li><li>Multiple angles if needed</li><li>Before/after comparisons</li><li>Annotations if you want them</li><li>Files ready for print or web</li></ul>
